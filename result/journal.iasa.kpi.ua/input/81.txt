Taylor series with a fuzzy complex number as an argument</h3></div><div><em>Igor Ya. Spectorsky</em></div><br><div><h4>Abstract</h4><br><div><p>This article considers functional sequences<em>ƒ<sub>n</sub>(A)</em>with fuzzy complex number<em>A</em>for an argument. The convergences lim<em><sub>n→∞</sub>ƒ'<sub>n</sub>(z)=ƒ(z)</em>and lim<em><sub>n→∞</sub>ƒ'<sub>n</sub>(x)=ƒ'(x)</em>are assumed to be uniform inside each circle<em>supp A</em>. Due to analyticity, the conditions of point-wise convergence of derivatives and finiteness of the number of solutions for equation<em>ƒ(z)=w</em>with respect to<em>z</em>for each<em>w</em>inside each circle<em>supp A</em>are satisfied. The paper proposes the sufficient conditions for the convergence<em>ƒ<sub>n</sub>(A)</em>in the sense that the sequence of membership functions μ<em><sub>ƒ<sub>n</sub>(A)</sub>(w)</em>converges point-wise. The convergence lim<em><sub>n→∞</sub>μ<sub>ƒ<sub>n</sub>(A)</sub>(w)=μ<sub>ƒ(A)</sub>(w)</em>is proved for all points<em>w∈X</em>, except such<em>w=ƒ(z)</em>, that<em>z</em>is a discontinuity point of μ<em><sub>A</sub>(z)</em>, or<em>ƒ'(z)=0</em>. As a particular case of a sequence<em>ƒ<sub>n</sub>(A)</em>, the generalization of Taylor series<em>ƒ(z)=∑<sub>i=0</sub><sup>∞</sup>ƒ<sup>(i)</sup>(z<sub>0</sub>)/i!(z-z<sub>0</sub>)<sup>i</sup></em>is considered for an analytical function<em>ƒ(z)</em>for the case of fuzzy complex argument<em>z=A</em>. The convergence of the series is considered in the sense of point-wise convergence of the partial sum μ<em><sub>S<sub>n</sub>(A)</sub>(w)</em>, where<em>S<sub>n</sub>(z)=∑<sub>i=0</sub><sup>∞</sup>ƒ<sup>(i)</sup>(z<sub>0</sub>)/i!(z-z<sub>0</sub>)<sup>i</sup></em>.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>fuzzy complex number;functional sequence;convergence</div><br></div><div><h4>Full Text:</h4><a href="http://journal.iasa.kpi.ua/article/view/59886/71661">PDF (Русский)</a></div><div><h4>References</h4><br><div><p>Orlovskij S.A.
