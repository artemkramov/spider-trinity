Liashenko, Doctor of Sciences (Economics), Professor Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv, Kyiv, Ukraine MODELLING OF ECONOMIC GROWTH WITH LEARNING BY DOING AND KNOWLEDGE SPILLOVERS</h1><div>Posted on<a href="http://bulletin-econom.univ.kiev.ua/archives/7621" title="09:23 AM">Wednesday June 1st, 2016</a><span>by<span><a href="http://bulletin-econom.univ.kiev.ua/archives/author/maxim" title="View all posts by Maxim">Maxim</a></span></span></div><div><p>A generalized parametric model as a modified Romer model is considered. This model describes the dependence of the output of the index of knowledge available to the firm. Result of this paper is a family of models between the two extremes – the neoclassical<span></span>model of decentralized type and nonstationary AK-model. The model assumes that all discoveries are unexpected by-products of the investment and that these discoveries instantly become common knowledge.</p><p>Keywords: economic growth; knowledge spillovers; AK-model; production function; endogenous growth.</p><p>Date of submission 25.02.2016</p><p>DOI:&nbsp;<a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.17721/1728-2667.2016/181-4/2">http://dx.doi.org/10.17721/1728-2667.2016/181-4/2</a></p><p><strong>References</strong></p><ol><li>Ramsey, F., 1928.
