Laval, economist West University of Timisoara, Timisoara, Romania THE MEASURABILITY OF CONTROLLING PERFORMANCE</h1><div>Posted on<a href="http://bulletin-econom.univ.kiev.ua/archives/8208" title="12:57 PM">Sunday April 30th, 2017</a><span>by<span><a href="http://bulletin-econom.univ.kiev.ua/archives/author/maxim" title="View all posts by Maxim">Maxim</a></span></span></div><div><p>The urge to increase the performance of company processes is ongoing. Surveys indicate however, that many companies do not measure the controlling performance with a defined set of key performance<span></span>indicators. This paper will analyze three categories of controlling key performance indicators based on their degree of measurability and their impact on the financial performance of a company. Potential measures to optimize the performance of the controlling department will be outlined and put in a logical order. The aligning of the controlling activity with the respective management expectation will be discussed as a key success factor of this improvement project.</p><p>Keywords: controlling; management accounting, controlling performance indicators, controlling role model.</p><p>Date of submission 04.01.2017</p><p>DOI:<a href="https://doi.org/10.17721/1728-2667.2017/191-2/4">https://doi.org/10.17721/1728-2667.2017/191-2/4</a></p><p><strong>References</strong></p><ol><li>Heimel, J.
