SYSTEMS</h3></div><div><em>Sergey Smolskiy, Maxim Generalov</em></div><br><div><h4>Abstract</h4><br><div><p>The processes of useful signal formation in the short-range radar systems constructed on the heterodyne, homodyne and autodyne&nbsp;principles are considered. The mathematical fundamentals of the fulfilled analysis are described in brief. It is shown that&nbsp;the autodyne configuration is much more complicated for the examination compared to the heterodyne and homodyne configurations&nbsp;due to the necessity of taking into account its own re-reflected signal. To simplify the theoretical analysis, the examination&nbsp;of the autodyne effect is performed under an assumption of reflected signal smallness. The comparison results of the considered&nbsp;configurations, which show that the homodyne and autodyne signal processing in the usual Doppler short-range radar&nbsp;turns out similar, are given. However, the autodyne short-range radar configuration itself has its own specific peculiarities,&nbsp;which should be taken into consideration in practice. It is proved that in the autodyne configuration one can meet the accompanying&nbsp;frequency modulation of the probing oscillation. It appears even at the absence of the forced frequency modulation,&nbsp;which is widely used to improve the short-range radar noise immunity. It is found out that this accompanying frequency modulation&nbsp;at weak reflected signals does not affect noticeably on the autodyne converter operation. The main theoretical conclusions&nbsp;of this paper are in the good conformity with the published results.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Full Text:</h4><a href="http://infotelesc.kpi.ua/article/view/29985/26841">PDF</a></div><div><h4>References</h4><br><div><p>Kogan I.
