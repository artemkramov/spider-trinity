Zhogov</em></div><br><div><h4>Abstract</h4><br><div><p><strong>Background.</strong>Energy harvesting is the actual topic. The system for energy harvesting has many components&nbsp;which require complex designing.<br><strong>Objective.</strong>The aim of the paper is optimal ways of collecting and accumulation of electromagnetic energy.<br><strong>Methods.</strong>Analysis of all known publications devoted to the energy harvesting, and induction of optimal&nbsp;elements for the energy harvester.<br><strong>Results.</strong>Optimal solutions and some practical implementations were proposed for each element of the energy&nbsp;harvester. Moreover, IR-UWB signals were proposed as a candidate for energy effective communications.<br><strong>Conclusion.</strong>Energy harvesting can be effectively used for power supply for some devices (for instance,<br>sensors). Also, energy effective communications (for instance, IR-UWB) can be used in those devices.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>energy harvesting; UWB; sensors; supercapacitors</div><br></div><div><h4>Full Text:</h4><a href="http://infotelesc.kpi.ua/article/view/82050/77698">PDF</a></div><div><h4>References</h4><br><div><p>GSMA Mobile Economy 2015 (2015) Retrieved from</p><p>http://www.gsmamobileeconomy.com/GSMA_Global_ Mobile_Economy_Report_2015.pdf</p><p>Arash Asadi, Qing Wang, Vincenzo Mancuso, A Survey on Device-to-Device Communication in Cellular Networks // IEEE COMMUNICATION</p><p>SURVEYS &amp; TUTORIALS, VOL. 16, NO. 4, FOURTH QUARTER 2014</p><p>S.
