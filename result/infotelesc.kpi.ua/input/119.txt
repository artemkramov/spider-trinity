NETWORKS</h3></div><div><em>Maxime Guirnyk</em></div><br><div><h4>Abstract</h4><br><div><p>Cooperative communication can improve the performance of cellular mobile networks. The optimization techniques based on&nbsp;duality theory, decomposition and subgradient method were applied for such wireless cellular cooperative systems. It was&nbsp;shown that the joint optimization and resource allocation problem can be solved efficiently within a network utility maximization&nbsp;framework. A concept of proportional fairness was used to achieve fair distribution of quality of service among users. Simulation&nbsp;results confirm the validity of the theoretical work.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Full Text:</h4><a href="http://infotelesc.kpi.ua/article/view/30000/26840">PDF</a></div><div><h4>References</h4><br><div><p>Nosratinia A., Hunter T.
