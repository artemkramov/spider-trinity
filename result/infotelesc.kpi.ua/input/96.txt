METHOD</h3></div><div><em>Vladislav Noskov, Kirill Ignatkov, Sergey Smolskiy</em></div><br><div><h4>Abstract</h4><br><div><p>The research results of oscillator internal parameters influence the features of dynamic autodyne characteristics formation in&nbsp;the case of external oscillator signal influence are presented. The equivalent circuit with a single-circuit oscillating system is&nbsp;considered as a model of the autodyne oscillator. Abbreviated equations are obtained by an averaging method and then they are<br>linearized for small disturbances in a vicinity of the steady-state mode. The obtained characteristics for the beating mode are&nbsp;compared with characteristics of autodynes for short-range radar technology. The essential differences in behavior of the oscillator&nbsp;with acting the external oscillator and the oscillator with acting the own reflected signal have been found. The physical&nbsp;sense of the frequency dispersion phenomenon for the autodyne frequency deviation in the vicinity of hypothetical “zero” beating&nbsp;is discovered. The research results of dynamic autodyne characteristics in the frequency conversion mode of signals modulated&nbsp;on amplitude or frequency are given. It is shown that to suppress the spurious harmonics of the beating frequency, it is&nbsp;advisable to take additional measures for generated frequency stabilization in autodyne frequency converters, for instance, using&nbsp;the external feedback in the oscillator or using the external high-Q resonator. The adequacy of theoretical conclusions is<br>confirmed by results of experimental investigations of the hybrid-integrated module of 8 mm-range made on the basis of the&nbsp;planar two-meza Gunn diode. Oscillator characteristics obtained by the beating method are compared with results of investigation&nbsp;fulfilled with the help of modulation characteristics. It is shown that errors in experimental determination of dynamic&nbsp;characteristics of autodyne oscillators caused by frequency limitations of a pin-diode typical for the modulation characteristic&nbsp;method can be eliminated. Problems of practical application of obtained results in real radar systems using autodyne oscillators<br>are discussed.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Full Text:</h4><a href="http://infotelesc.kpi.ua/article/view/30199/26910">PDF</a></div><div><h4>References</h4><br><div><p>Rocket F.
