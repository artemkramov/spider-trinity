href="http://visnyk.soc.univ.kiev.ua/index.php/soc/article/view/37/0">Bureychak</a></div><div><div></div><div><h3>Gender (non-)equality in the labor market in the Crimea: Indicators, evaluation and interpretation</h3></div><div><em>T. Bureychak, T. Martsenyuk, D. Hutky</em></div><br><div><h4>Abstract</h4><br><div><p>The article discusses parity in participation of women and men in the labour market, which is a key indicator of gender equality. The aim of this paper is to elaborate a complex approach for analysis of gender aspects of labour market, which is applied on example of the Autonomous Republic of Crimea. In particular, we offered a complex examination of the situation based on statistical indicators, expert interviews and interpretations by the population (based on representative opinion poll). Combination of these methods also allows triangulation of the data. The study corroborates prevalence of men's advantages in their access to resources in the labour market as well as their institutional privileges. Men to a smaller extend in contrast to women realize facts of women's discrimination and to a larger extend reproduce gender stereotypes, which legitimize their privileges. This study also demonstrates that gender inequality in the labour sphere has complex, intersectional and multilevel character. It is evidenced in vertical and horizontal dimensions, imbalance in combining work and parental responsibilities by women and men, as well as pervasiveness of gender discrimination of women.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>gender; gender inequality; gender discrimination; labour market</div><br></div><div><h4>Full Text:</h4><a href="http://visnyk.soc.univ.kiev.ua/index.php/soc/article/view/37/23">PDF (Українська)</a></div><div><h4>References</h4><br><div><p>Ekonomichna akty`vnist` naselennya za stattyu ta miscem prozhy`vannya u 2010 roci.
